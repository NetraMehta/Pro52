Camel

  • Camels' ears are furry
  • Camels can move easily on the sand because of their specially designed feet
  • When camels find water, they will drink as much as possible

Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin colour is an important part of communication among chameleons
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Flying-Cicida

  • Cicidas are insects in the order Hemiptera
  • Cicadas have prominent eyes set wide apart, short antennae, and membranous front wings
  • Cicidas have an exceptionally loud song, produced in most species by the rapid buckling and unbuckling of drumlike tymbals

Dog

  • Dogs are a decendant of an extinct species of wolf
  • Dogs have helped people in hunting, herding, pulling loads, protection, assisting police and the military, companionship, therapy, and aiding disabled people.
  • Because of that, dogs have been given the title of 'Man's Best Friend'

Frog

  • Frogs are a part of the order Anura
  • Frogs are a carnivorous group of short-bodied, tailless amphibians
  • Frogs lay their eggs in water

Panda

  • Pandas are a type of bear
  • Pandas are also called Giant Pandas
  • Pandas are native to China

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are part of the order Columbiformes
  • Pigeons are stout-bodied birds with short necks, and short slender bills that in some species feature fleshy ceres
  • In English, the smaller species tend to be called "doves" and the larger ones "pigeons"

Teddy-Bear

  • Teddy bears are stuffed toys in the form of bears
  • Since the creation of the first teddy bears which sought to imitate the form of real bear cubs, "teddies" have greatly varied in form, style, color, and material
  • Teddy bears are among the most popular gifts for children and are often given to adults to signify love, affection, congratulations, or sympathy
